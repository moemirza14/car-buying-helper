Negotiation Prep

Research first. Talk price, not payments. Walk away if it doesn't feel right.

Before you go (research)
  • Know the invoice price. Use Kelley Blue Book, Edmunds, and TrueCar to find what dealers paid. Aim 2–5% above invoice on a new car.
  • Get pre-approved financing from your bank or credit union before visiting. Dealer financing should beat this rate to win your business.
  • Research market value of your trade-in separately (KBB private party value is higher than trade-in value).
  • Email 3–5 dealers with the exact trim/options you want. Ask for an "out-the-door" price. Use lower offers as leverage.
  • Time it right. End of month, end of quarter, and end of year are when dealers chase quotas.
At the dealership
  • Negotiate the out-the-door price, not the monthly payment. Monthly-payment negotiation hides longer loans and add-ons.
  • Keep trade-in, financing, and price separate. Dealers shift profit between these — solve them one at a time.
  • Decline the F&I office extras (extended warranty, paint sealant, GAP insurance, VIN etching) unless you've priced them elsewhere. You can buy GAP from your insurer for much less.
  • Read every line of the contract. Watch for "doc fees" above $200, fake "market adjustments," and pre-checked add-ons.
  • Be ready to walk away. If a deal isn't right, leave. There will be another car at another dealer.
Red flag phrases: "What payment do you want to be at?" — "We can only do this deal today." — "Let me check with my manager" (theatrical anchoring). Stay focused on the out-the-door number.

Used Car Inspection

A 20-minute walkaround can save you thousands. Always get a pre-purchase inspection from an independent mechanic.

Documents (do this first)
  • Vehicle history report (Carfax or AutoCheck) — look for accidents, salvage title, odometer rollback, and number of owners.
  • Match the VIN on the dashboard, door jamb, and title — they must all match.
  • Service records — regular oil changes and dealer stamps are a green flag.
Exterior walkaround
  • Check panel gaps — uneven gaps suggest prior collision repair.
  • Paint mismatch under sunlight or runs/orange peel texture = repaint after an accident.
  • Tires: even tread wear across all four. Uneven wear = alignment or suspension issues.
  • Rust on frame, subframe, or rocker panels is a deal-breaker. Surface rust on bolts is normal.
Under the hood
  • Milky oil cap = head gasket failure. Walk away.
  • Brown/burnt transmission fluid (should be pink/red) = neglected maintenance.
  • No oil leaks on the ground or engine bay.
  • Battery terminals clean, no corrosion.
Test drive
  • Cold start — listen for knocking, ticking, or grinding.
  • Steering wheel pulls left or right when hands off = alignment issue (cheap) or frame damage (expensive).
  • Vibration at 60+ mph = warped rotors or tire balance issue.
  • Transmission slipping, harsh shifts, or delayed engagement on a 5+ year old car = potentially $3k–6k repair.
  • All electronics work: windows, locks, AC, heat, infotainment, backup camera.
  • Check engine light on or recently cleared (no engine codes when scanned) = something is being hidden.
Always: Get a pre-purchase inspection (PPI) at an independent mechanic before signing. Costs $100–200 and routinely catches $1,000+ problems. If the seller refuses a PPI, walk away.
